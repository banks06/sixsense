HOW IT WORKS

Six Sense is a wearable safety device built for the walk to the car, the solo run at dusk, the campus path with too few streetlights. Instead of a camera watching everything, it uses privacy-preserving radar to read movement patterns around you in real time, noticing when someone starts closing distance or matching your route, and surfaces that awareness before it would otherwise register.

No footage. No facial recognition. No one watching a live feed. Six Sense measures how people and objects move, not who they are — so protection doesn't come at the cost of privacy, for you or for anyone nearby.

DETECT

Radar sensing picks up closing distance and persistent following patterns. The situational shift you might not register until it's too late.

ALERT

A quiet, immediate notification the moment something changes. Enough time to cross the street, call someone, or just be ready.

PROTECT

Continuous on-device monitoring that reads motion, not video, so protection never means being under surveillance.

CONNECT

One-tap SOS and live location sharing with trusted contacts turn an alert into actual help, fast, without fumbling for a phone.

Six Sense watches movement, not people.

No continuous video. No stored footage. Just faster awareness when it matters.
